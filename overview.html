History

Chess, originating in India as "Chaturanga" around the 6th century, evolved through Persia and Europe into its modern form by the 15th century.

Game Rules

The game is played on an 8x8 board with 16 pieces per player: 1 king, 1 queen, 2 rooks, 2 bishops, 2 knights, and 8 pawns. The objective is to checkmate the opponentâ€™s king. Pieces move uniquely: the king one square in any direction, the queen any number of squares in any direction, the rook horizontally or vertically, the bishop diagonally, the knight in an L-shape, and the pawn forward with special moves like en passant and promotion.
